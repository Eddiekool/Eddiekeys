Who Is IPC

The International Preschool Curriculum (IPC).

IPC is a Professional Association, with its headquaters in the United States of America. It was founded to strenthen and harmonize early childhood education standards by ofering a unique set of products and services that promote active learning and effective practices in teaching.

IPC serves schools, Learners, Parents and governments. IPC enjoys global recognition and works closely with governments to amen curricula materials required to ensure local standard compliance...

The IPC currently oversees approximately three new school openings per month and is currently advising more than twenty corporations or individuals who are in the process of opening a preschool. Since the inception, the IPC has been involved in all aspects of general education consultancy including school management, licensing, legal assistance and teaching training.

As the world leader in international early childhood education, the IPC can assist you in establishing your own school or provide the established school owner or board with tailored school services.

With over 300 prospective and full members and with representation on 6 continents, the IPC has established an authoritative position in the field of international early childhood education. Since the launch of the preschool Franchise program in 2010, IPC Quasi Franchises have been granted to preschools in the United states of Amwerica, United Arab Emirates, India, Thailand, Myanmar, Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, Malawi, Kazakhstan, Oman, Malaysia and Nigeria.

All IPC Franchises and licensed schools receive teacher training (maximum enrolment capacities apply). The training course is advised by our own

ex Harvard University teaching fellow

and is delivered online over a 6 month period. The course develops essential teaching skills and ensures that the curriculum is taught in its intended manner.

  • Greater awareness for current teaching practices
  • Understanding of professionalism and ethics
  • Improved teaching quality
  • Quality assurance for parents and community
  • Development of very appropriate for 6 months to 6 years old
